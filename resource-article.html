The Sleep-Recovery Connection

Why recovery quality matters more than training volume for most members — and what to actually do about it.

Most members who plateau aren't undertraining. They're under-recovering — and sleep is the single biggest lever most people have never adjusted on purpose.

What we actually track

We don't ask members to obsess over sleep scores. Instead, our coaches watch two things: consistency of bedtime, and how a member's first working set feels compared to their baseline. Both are simple, and both tell you more than a wearable's proprietary "recovery score."

Three changes that move the needle

  • A consistent wake time, even on rest days
  • Caffeine cut off eight hours before bed, not just after lunch
  • One wind-down cue — the same one, every night
You can't out-train a bad sleep schedule. You can only postpone the bill.

When to bring it to your physician

If sleep issues persist for more than a few weeks despite consistent habits, that's worth a conversation with your physician rather than another app. It's often addressed faster than people expect.
